Terms From Leadership

Computer Science

Terms

Computational Thinking: Computational thinking allows us to take a complex problem, understand what the problem is and develop possible solutions. 4 keys: Decomposition, Pattern recognition, abstraction, algorithm.

Programming:

The action or process of writing computer programs.

Decomposition:

Taking a complex problem and breaking it down into smaller more manageable problems.The smaller parts can then be examined and solved, or designed individually, as they are simpler to work with.

Pattern Recognition:

Finding problems and similarities among small problems that were previously decomposed.

Abtraction:

Focusing only on the important details, while ignoring irrelevant information. Allows us to create a general idea of what the problem is and how to solve it.

Algorithms:

Create simple steps or rules to solve each of the smaller problems can be designed.

PsudoCode:

A notation resembling a simplified programming language.

Flow Charts:

A flowchart is a type of diagram that represents a workflow or process. A flowchart can also be defined as a diagrammatic representation of an algorithm.

Evaluation:

The making of a judgment about the amount, number, or value of something.

Debugging:

The process of identifying and removing errors from computer hardware or software.

Sequence, Selection & Repetition/Structured programming:

Structured programming is a program written with only the structured programming constructions: (1) sequence, (2) repetition, and (3) selection.

Digitial Divide:

The gulf between those who have ready access to computers and the Internet, and those who do not.

Logical Thinking:

Logical thinking is the process in which one uses reasoning consistently to come to a conclusion.

Critial Thinking:

Critical thinking is the analysis of facts to form a judgment.

Creative Thinking:

A way of looking at problems or situations from a fresh perspective that suggests unorthodox solutions.

Problem Solving:

Finding solutions to a difficult or complex issue.

Desktop:

The first GUI you see when you turn on your computer.

Menu Bar:

Holds things based on the applications. Allows us to manipulate an application.

Launchpad:

Holds all your applications.

Dock:

Hold applications and files that are open.

Finder:

Holds all files and folders.

Extensions:

Additional information about a file that is added to the end of a file name.

Directories:

Container that hold files and other directories.

Window:

Some that displays the applications content.

Root:

The folder that holds all other folders.
